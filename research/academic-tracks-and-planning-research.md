## 2. Standardized Testing Landscape (2025-2026)

### The Big Shift: Tests Are Back

[Multiple top schools have reinstated SAT/ACT requirements](https://www.carnegieprep.com/college-admissions/college-admissions-testing-policy-updates/) for 2025-2026 admissions:
- **Reinstated**: Harvard, Yale, Brown, Dartmouth, Cornell, Stanford, Caltech, UT Austin, UPenn, U of Miami
- **Still test-optional**: Columbia, Princeton (as of 2026)
- **Key takeaway**: Even test-optional schools' data shows submitting scores helps. Plan to test.

### SAT vs ACT: Which One?

[Neither is inherently harder](https://www.princetonreview.com/college/sat-act) - different students suit different tests:

| Feature | SAT | ACT |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Format** | Digital only, adaptive | Paper or online, linear |
| **Duration** | 2 hours 14 min | 2 hours 55 min (with writing) |
| **Sections** | Reading/Writing + Math | English, Math, Reading, Science (+optional Writing) |
| **Math** | Calculator always available, formulas provided, ~1:35/question | No formulas provided, ~1:07/question, more topics (matrices, logs) |
| **Science** | Integrated into reading/math | Separate science section |
| **Scoring** | 400-1600 | 1-36 composite |
| **Best for** | Deliberate thinkers, strong math reasoning | Fast readers, science-strong students, straightforward question lovers |

**Recommendation**: [Take a practice test of each](https://www.kaptest.com/sat/sat-vs-act) by sophomore spring. Go with whichever feels more natural.

### Testing Timeline

| When | What |
|------|------|
| **Sophomore Fall** | Take PSAT (practice + National Merit qualifier) |
| **Sophomore Spring** | Take one practice SAT and one practice ACT |
| **Junior Fall** | Begin targeted prep for chosen test |
| **Junior Spring** | First official SAT or ACT (March-June) |
| **Junior Summer** | Retake prep if needed |
| **Senior Fall** | Final retake if needed (most colleges accept Oct/Nov scores) |

### AP Exams

[AP exams are scored 1-5](https://apstudents.collegeboard.org/courses). A score of 3+ is generally "passing," but many competitive colleges want 4-5 for credit.

**Notable**: [Yale now accepts AP/IB scores instead of SAT/ACT](https://www.thecollegesage.com/blog/2025/6/10/the-return-of-test-requirements-colleges-that-now-require-sat-or-act-scores), signaling growing AP importance.

## 3. Core Math & Science Sequences

### Math Pathways

[The standard sequence](https://blog.collegevine.com/high-school-math-classes) and accelerated options:

| Year | Standard Track | Accelerated Track | STEM-Bound Track |
|------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **9th** | Algebra I | Geometry | Algebra II / Honors Geometry |
| **10th** | Geometry | Algebra II | Pre-Calculus Honors |
| **11th** | Algebra II | Pre-Calculus | AP Calculus AB or BC |
| **12th** | Pre-Calculus | AP Calculus AB | AP Calculus BC, AP Statistics, or Multivariable Calculus |

**Which math for which major**:
- **Engineering/Physics/CS**: Must reach Calculus (BC preferred). [AP Calculus BC covers a full year of college calc](https://collegeprep.uworld.com/ap/best-online-courses-and-classes/).
- **Pre-Med/Bio**: Calculus AB or BC + Statistics recommended
- **Business/Economics**: Calculus AB + AP Statistics strongly recommended
- **Humanities/Social Science**: Through Pre-Calculus is sufficient; AP Statistics is valuable
- **Arts/Design**: Through Algebra II minimum; Statistics if pursuing design thinking

### Science Pathways

| Year | General College Prep | STEM Track | Pre-Med Track |
|------|---------------------|------------|--------------|
| **9th** | Biology | Honors Biology | Honors Biology |
| **10th** | Chemistry | Honors Chemistry | Honors Chemistry |
| **11th** | Physics | AP Physics 1 or AP Chemistry | AP Chemistry |
| **12th** | Elective/AP Environmental | AP Physics C and/or AP Bio | AP Biology + AP Physics |

## 4. Academic Tracks: Detailed Breakdown

### STEM: Engineering

**Goal**: Top engineering programs (MIT, Stanford, Georgia Tech, Purdue, etc.)

**Must-Have Courses**:
- [AP Calculus BC](https://blog.collegevine.com/ap-classes-for-engineering) (most important single course)
- [AP Physics C: Mechanics and/or E&M](https://prepexpert.com/ap-classes-engineering/) (mirrors college physics for engineers)
- AP Chemistry
- AP Computer Science A (increasingly expected)

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP Statistics
- Multivariable Calculus or Linear Algebra (dual enrollment)
- AP Computer Science Principles

**Extracurriculars**:
- [FIRST Robotics](https://www.create-learn.us/blog/stem-competitions-and-contests/) or VEX Robotics
- Science Olympiad
- [Math competitions: AMC 10/12 → AIME](https://www.immerse.education/academic-competitions/stem/stem-competitions-for-high-school-students/)
- Engineering summer programs ([MIT MITES](https://www.successkoach.com/engineering-and-cs-prep), MIT Beaver Works)
- Personal engineering/building projects
- Research with a professor or mentor

**Tests**: SAT/ACT with strong math score (770+ SAT Math, 34+ ACT Math ideal for top programs)

---

### STEM: Computer Science

**Must-Have Courses**:
- AP Computer Science A (Java-based, core)
- AP Calculus BC
- AP Statistics
- AP Physics 1 or C

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP Computer Science Principles
- Data Science or AI courses (if available)
- Discrete Mathematics (dual enrollment)

**Extracurriculars**:
- [USACO (USA Computing Olympiad)](https://www.omegalearn.org/high-school-competition-coding) - most prestigious CS competition
- Hackathons (MLH, local/school events)
- [Coding competitions: CodeWars, IOI](https://blog.prepscholar.com/computer-science-competitions-for-high-schoolers)
- Open source contributions on GitHub
- Personal coding projects / apps / websites
- Cybersecurity competitions (CyberPatriot, picoCTF)
- CS summer programs (Stanford AI4ALL, Carnegie Mellon programs)

**Tests**: SAT/ACT + strong AP CS A score

---

### STEM: Pre-Med / Health Sciences

**Goal**: Strong GPA + science foundation for college pre-med track → medical school

**Must-Have Courses**:
- [AP Biology](https://blog.collegevine.com/which-aps-med-and-stem-programs) (top priority - overlaps with MCAT)
- [AP Chemistry](https://hsafp.org/best-high-school-courses-and-activities-for-pre-med-students/) (essential for organic chem in college)
- AP Psychology (directly relevant to MCAT psych/soc section)
- AP Statistics (used heavily in medical research)
- AP Calculus AB minimum

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP Physics 1 (MCAT includes physics)
- Anatomy & Physiology (if available)
- [Spanish](https://www.inspiraadvantage.com/blog/how-to-prepare-for-pre-med-in-high-school) (most useful language for US healthcare)
- AP English Language (communication skills)
- Public Speaking / Debate

**Extracurriculars**:
- [Hospital volunteering / Junior volunteer programs](https://www.medschoolcoach.com/extracurriculars-for-premeds/)
- [Physician shadowing](https://www.inspiraadvantage.com/blog/how-to-prepare-for-pre-med-in-high-school)
- [Research internships](https://blog.prepscholar.com/medical-programs-for-high-school-students) at hospitals or universities
- Health-related certifications ([CNA, EMT](https://www.auamed.org/blog/how-to-prepare-for-pre-med-in-high-school/))
- Science competitions (Science Olympiad, science fair)
- HOSA (Health Occupations Students of America)
- Crisis hotline volunteering
- Global health / medical mission trips

**Tests**: SAT/ACT + AP scores of 4-5 in science APs

**Important note**: Medical school is a marathon. High school sets the foundation, but the real pre-med grind is in college. Focus on building genuine interest and strong study habits, not burning out early.

### Law / Political Science / Humanities

**Must-Have Courses**:
- [AP U.S. History + AP World History](https://blog.collegevine.com/lawyer-extracurriculars-and-classes) (historical analysis and writing)
- AP English Language AND AP English Literature (reading and writing mastery)
- AP U.S. Government and Politics
- AP Comparative Government (if available)

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP European History
- AP Psychology or AP Human Geography
- AP Statistics (data literacy)
- Philosophy or Ethics courses
- Latin or foreign language (4 years recommended)
- [Public Speaking / Debate courses](https://jdadvising.com/how-to-prepare-for-law-school-as-a-high-school-student/)

**Extracurriculars**:
- [Mock Trial](https://ingeniusprep.com/blog/high-school-students-interested-in-law/) (top choice - simulates legal reasoning)

- School newspaper / journalism
- Community advocacy / volunteering
- Political campaigns or civic organizations
- Writing competitions
- Youth court / teen court programs

**Tests**: SAT/ACT with strong reading/writing scores + AP Humanities scores

**Important note**: There is no "pre-law" major in college. Law schools accept all majors. The key skills are reading comprehension, analytical writing, and logical reasoning - which is what the LSAT tests.

### Trades / Vocational / CTE Pathways

**This is NOT a lesser path.** [Skilled trades earn $55,680 median](https://www.usnews.com/education/best-colleges/articles/a-guide-to-trade-schools-and-vocational-programs) (above national median), and many trades earn [$65,000-$100,000+](https://www.skillcatapp.com/post/best-trade-schools-2025-programs-costs-careers).

**High School CTE Programs** ([Career and Technical Education](https://www.usnews.com/education/k12/articles/the-benefits-of-career-and-technical-education-programs-for-high-schoolers)):
- CTE programs cover 16 career clusters: Agriculture, Architecture, Arts/AV, Business, Education, Finance, Government, Health Science, Hospitality, Human Services, IT, Law/Public Safety, Manufacturing, Marketing, STEM, Transportation
- Many lead to industry certifications before graduation
- Some include paid apprenticeships

**Popular Trade Paths**:
| Trade | Training Time | Median Salary | Path |
|-------|--------------|---------------|------|
| Electrician | 4-5 years (apprenticeship) | $65,000+ | CTE → Apprenticeship |
| Plumber | 4-5 years (apprenticeship) | $60,000+ | CTE → Apprenticeship |
| HVAC Technician | 6 months - 2 years | $55,000+ | CTE → Trade school |
| Welder | 7 months - 2 years | $50,000+ | CTE → Certification |
| Dental Hygienist | 2-3 years | $80,000+ | CTE → Associate degree |
| EMT/Paramedic | 6 months - 2 years | $45,000-65,000 | CTE → Certification |
| Automotive Tech | 1-2 years | $50,000+ | CTE → Trade school |
| Cybersecurity Tech | 1-2 years | $60,000+ | CTE → Certification |

**Advantages over 4-year college**:
- Little to no student debt
- Start earning 2-4 years earlier
- High demand (labor shortages in many trades)
- Hands-on work vs. classroom
- Many trades lead to self-employment/business ownership

## 5. Dual Enrollment / Community College

[Dual enrollment](https://www.act.org/content/act/en/students-and-parents/high-school-success/high-school-resources/dual-enrollment-faqs.html) lets students take college courses while in high school:

**Benefits**:
- Earn transferable college credit (often free or reduced cost)
- Experience college-level academic rigor
- Explore potential majors early
- [Boosts GPA weighting](https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/counselors/preparing-freshman-students/dual-enrollment.html) at some schools (UC system gives extra GPA points)
- Can fill gaps when school doesn't offer advanced courses
- Shows initiative to admissions officers

**Best uses**:
- When your school doesn't offer a course you need (e.g., Multivariable Calculus, advanced CS)
- To explore a field before committing (e.g., take an intro philosophy course)
- To get ahead on general education requirements
- When you've exhausted AP options in your area of interest

**Important**: [Check transfer policies](https://collegesofdistinction.com/advice/how-to-transfer-dual-enrollment-credits-to-college/) - most public universities accept dual enrollment credits, but policies vary by school and state.

## 6. The Extracurricular Strategy

### Depth > Breadth

[Top colleges want "well-lopsided," not "well-rounded"](https://www.crimsoneducation.org/us/blog/top-colleges-dont-want-well-rounded-students-they-want-specialists):

- **The old model**: Join 10 clubs, do a bit of everything
- **The new model**: Deep commitment to 2-3 areas with demonstrated impact and growth

[NACAC data confirms](https://www.cirkledin.com/library/extracurricular-activities-and-leadership/college-application-spike-well-rounded-strategy/): admissions officers prefer **sustained engagement + leadership + measurable impact** over a long list of memberships.

### Building a "Spike"

A spike is [deep, demonstrated expertise in one area](https://blog.collegevine.com/need-extracurricular-spike):

1. **Choose 1-2 core activities** aligned with your interest/track
2. **Show progression**: Member → Leader → Founder/Competitor/Creator
3. **Create impact**: Start something, win something, change something
4. **Be authentic**: The spike should reflect genuine passion, not resume padding

### Extracurricular Tiers (from most to least impressive)

| Tier | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Rare, national/international achievement | Intel Science Fair winner, USAMO qualifier, published research, national team |
| **Tier 2** | High achievement, significant leadership | Student body president, state competition winner, founded organization with measurable impact |
| **Tier 3** | Meaningful involvement with some distinction | Club officer, varsity sport captain, Eagle Scout, regional competition finalist |
| **Tier 4** | Solid participation | Club member, volunteer work, part-time job, school sports |

Most students have Tier 3-4 activities. Even one Tier 2 activity sets you apart.

## 8. Grade-by-Grade Master Planning Guide

### Freshman Year (9th Grade)

**Academic Focus**:
- Take honors-level courses where confident
- Build strong GPA foundation (cumulative GPA starts now and it's easier to maintain than recover)
- Explore electives to discover interests
- 4 core subjects + 1-2 exploratory electives

**Testing**: None required. Focus on learning and grades.

**Extracurriculars**:
- Join 2-3 activities aligned with emerging interests
- Try different things - this is exploration year
- No leadership pressure yet

**Key Actions**:
- Meet with school counselor to map 4-year plan
- Start thinking about what subjects excite you
- Build strong study habits and time management systems

---

### Sophomore Year (10th Grade)

**Academic Focus**:
- Increase rigor: 1-2 AP or Honors courses
- Good first AP options: AP Human Geography, AP World History, AP Computer Science Principles, AP Environmental Science
- Continue building core sequence (especially math)

**Testing**:
- Take PSAT in October (practice + National Merit qualifier)
- Take one practice SAT and one practice ACT to compare
- Consider AP exams if taking AP courses

**Extracurriculars**:
- Narrow from 3 to 2-3 core activities
- Seek increasing responsibility (committee chair, section leader)
- Begin summer planning: programs, jobs, or volunteer work

**Key Actions**:
- Start a college research list (casual browsing)
- Identify your emerging "spike"
- Plan junior year course load with counselor (this is the most important year for college apps)

---

### Junior Year (11th Grade)

**Academic Focus**:
- Peak rigor year: 3-4 AP courses recommended for competitive colleges
- Take APs that align with your intended track/major
- This year's grades matter MOST for college applications

**Testing**:
- SAT or ACT: First attempt in spring (March-June)
- AP exams in May
- SAT Subject Tests if required (rare now but check target schools)

**Extracurriculars**:
- Leadership roles in core activities
- Competitions and awards season
- Summer between junior-senior year: most important summer (research, internships, pre-college programs, meaningful projects)

**Key Actions**:
- Build college list (reach / match / safety)
- Visit campuses (virtual and in-person)
- Research financial aid and scholarships
- Start Common App brainstorming
- Request teacher recommendation letters (spring)

---

### Senior Year (12th Grade)

**Academic Focus**:
- Maintain rigor (don't slack off - colleges check senior grades)
- Continue AP courses in your track
- Senioritis is real - but admissions can be rescinded for grade drops

**Testing**:
- SAT/ACT retake if needed (fall)
- Final AP exams in May
- FAFSA opens October 1 - complete ASAP

**Extracurriculars**:
- Capstone projects or achievements in core activities
- Mentoring younger students
- Continue meaningful involvement (don't quit everything)

**Key Actions**:
- Early Decision/Early Action deadlines: November 1-15
- Regular Decision deadlines: January 1-15
- Financial aid and scholarship applications
- Final college decision by May 1
