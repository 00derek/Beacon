# Contributing to Beacon

Beacon is built on domain knowledge — counseling techniques, admissions expertise, student experiences, and educational frameworks. The most valuable contributions are ideas and insights, not code.

**You don't need to be technical to contribute.** If you have an idea that would help students, we want to hear it. Describe it in plain language, submit a GitHub Issue, and we'll use AI to integrate it into the right files.

---

## Two Ways to Contribute

### 1. Suggest (open an Issue)

Best for: counselors, students, parents, educators, or anyone with an insight.

Go to [Issues](../../issues/new/choose) and pick the template that fits:

| Template | Use when... |
|----------|------------|
| **Enrich a skill** | You know a framework, technique, or knowledge area that would make Beacon better |
| **Suggest a feature** | You have an idea for new functionality or a way to improve existing commands |
| **Share an experience** | You've used Beacon and noticed something that worked well or didn't |
| **Flag a gap or error** | You spotted incorrect information, a missing perspective, or an equity gap |

Fill in the fields, submit, and you're done. A maintainer will review your suggestion and handle the implementation.

### 2. Build (open a PR)

Best for: people comfortable editing markdown who want to implement changes directly.

1. Fork the repo
2. Create a branch (`your-username/short-description`)
3. Make your changes
4. Open a pull request with a clear description of what you changed and why

You can pick up open Issues labeled `help wanted` if you're looking for something to work on.

**Don't worry about getting the markdown perfect.** Beacon's reference files have specific structure, but clear thinking matters more than formatting. We'll work with you to get it right.

## What Lives Where

Beacon is organized by topic. You don't need to know exact file paths — just describe the area your contribution touches and we'll route it:

- **Interest discovery** — frameworks and techniques for helping students figure out what they care about
- **Academic planning** — course sequences, academic tracks, school-level planning
- **Activities & spike strategy** — extracurricular depth, leadership progression, activity ranking
- **Testing** — SAT/ACT strategy, AP exams, test-optional landscape
- **College list building** — school research, reach/match/safety, demonstrated interest
- **Essays** — Socratic coaching techniques, anonymized examples, narrative development
- **Applications** — ED/EA/RD strategy, recommendation letters, activity descriptions
- **Financial aid** — FAFSA, CSS Profile, scholarships, aid negotiation
- **Summer planning** — programs, projects, internships by grade level
- **Timeline** — milestone database, grade-aware deadlines, seasonal planning
- **Counselor style** — communication approaches, question framing, tone adaptation
- **Safety** — crisis detection, resource routing, equity checks
- **General** — cross-cutting techniques, motivational interviewing, overall approach

---

## Guidelines

- **Students first.** Every contribution should ultimately help a high school student navigate their path.
- **Equity matters.** All paths — trades, community college, gap years, military — deserve equal respect. Contributions that broaden access are especially welcome.
- **Socratic principle.** Beacon guides through questions, never writes content for students. Contributions should preserve this approach.
- **Evidence over opinion.** When suggesting frameworks or techniques, share where they come from — a research paper, professional experience, a specific program, etc.
