# academic-tracks — Academic Track Mapping and Course Sequences

This module maps student interests to concrete academic pathways: courses, testing priorities, extracurriculars, and year-by-year sequences. Loaded by `plan`, `activities`, `testing`, and `review`.

---

## Course Rigor Principle

Admissions officers evaluate whether you **challenged yourself relative to what was available**. A student taking 5 APs at a school offering 5 is viewed more favorably than a student taking 5 at a school offering 20. This means:

- Take the most rigorous courses your school offers in your areas of strength
- If your school lacks advanced courses, pursue dual enrollment
- A "B" in AP Chemistry demonstrates more than an "A" in regular Chemistry for a STEM-bound student
- Schools understand different high schools have different offerings — they evaluate context

When advising students, always ask: "What does your school offer?" before recommending specific courses. Adapt recommendations to what is actually available.

## Seven Academic Tracks

### STEM: Engineering

**Goal**: Top engineering programs (MIT, Stanford, Georgia Tech, Purdue, etc.)

**Must-Have Courses**:
- AP Calculus BC (most important single course for engineering)
- AP Physics C: Mechanics and/or E&M (mirrors college physics for engineers)
- AP Chemistry
- AP Computer Science A (increasingly expected)

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP Statistics
- Multivariable Calculus or Linear Algebra (dual enrollment if school doesn't offer)
- AP Computer Science Principles

**Recommended Extracurriculars**:
- FIRST Robotics or VEX Robotics
- Science Olympiad
- Math competitions: AMC 10/12, AIME
- Engineering summer programs (MIT MITES, MIT Beaver Works)
- Personal engineering/building projects
- Research with a professor or mentor

**Testing Priorities**: SAT/ACT with strong math (770+ SAT Math, 34+ ACT Math ideal for top programs). AP scores of 4-5 in STEM APs.

**Course Sequence**:

| Year | Standard Path | Accelerated Path |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **9th** | Honors Biology, Algebra I or Geometry | Honors Biology, Honors Geometry or Algebra II |
| **10th** | Honors Chemistry, Geometry or Algebra II | Honors Chemistry, Pre-Calculus Honors |
| **11th** | AP Physics 1 or AP Chemistry, Pre-Calculus | AP Physics C, AP Calculus BC, AP Chemistry |
| **12th** | AP Calculus AB, AP Physics C, AP CS A | AP Physics C: E&M, Multivariable Calc, AP CS A |

### STEM: Computer Science

**Must-Have Courses**:
- AP Computer Science A (Java-based, core)
- AP Calculus BC
- AP Statistics
- AP Physics 1 or C

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP Computer Science Principles
- Data Science or AI courses (if available)
- Discrete Mathematics (dual enrollment)

**Recommended Extracurriculars**:
- USACO (USA Computing Olympiad) — most prestigious CS competition
- Hackathons (MLH, local/school events)
- Coding competitions: CodeWars, IOI
- Open source contributions on GitHub
- Personal coding projects / apps / websites
- Cybersecurity competitions (CyberPatriot, picoCTF)
- CS summer programs (Stanford AI4ALL, Carnegie Mellon programs)

**Testing Priorities**: SAT/ACT with strong math + AP CS A score of 4-5.

**Course Sequence**:

| Year | Standard Path | Accelerated Path |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **9th** | Intro to CS or AP CSP, Algebra I/Geometry | AP CSP, Honors Geometry or Algebra II |
| **10th** | AP CS A, Geometry/Algebra II | AP CS A, Pre-Calculus Honors |
| **11th** | AP Statistics, Pre-Calculus, AP Physics 1 | AP Calculus BC, AP Statistics, AP Physics C |
| **12th** | AP Calculus AB/BC, Advanced CS elective | Data Structures (dual enrollment), Discrete Math |

### STEM: Pre-Med / Health Sciences

**Goal**: Strong GPA + science foundation for college pre-med track leading to medical school.

**Must-Have Courses**:
- AP Biology (top priority — overlaps with MCAT)
- AP Chemistry (essential for organic chemistry in college)
- AP Psychology (directly relevant to MCAT psych/soc section)
- AP Statistics (used heavily in medical research)
- AP Calculus AB minimum

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP Physics 1 (MCAT includes physics)
- Anatomy & Physiology (if available)
- Spanish (most useful language for US healthcare)
- AP English Language (communication skills)
- Public Speaking / Debate

**Recommended Extracurriculars**:
- Hospital volunteering / Junior volunteer programs
- Physician shadowing
- Research internships at hospitals or universities
- Health-related certifications (CNA, EMT)
- Science competitions (Science Olympiad, science fair)
- HOSA (Health Occupations Students of America)
- Crisis hotline volunteering
- Global health / medical mission trips

**Testing Priorities**: SAT/ACT + AP scores of 4-5 in science APs.

**Course Sequence**:

| Year | Standard Path | Accelerated Path |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **9th** | Honors Biology, Algebra I/Geometry | Honors Biology, Honors Geometry |
| **10th** | Honors Chemistry, Geometry/Algebra II | Honors Chemistry, Pre-Calculus Honors |
| **11th** | AP Chemistry, Pre-Calculus, AP Psychology | AP Chemistry, AP Biology, AP Calculus AB |
| **12th** | AP Biology, AP Calculus AB, AP Statistics | AP Physics 1, AP Statistics, AP Psychology |

**Important note**: Medical school is a marathon. High school sets the foundation, but the real pre-med grind is in college. Focus on building genuine interest and strong study habits, not burning out early.

### Business / Finance / Economics

**Must-Have Courses**:
- AP Macroeconomics + AP Microeconomics (core knowledge)
- AP Statistics (essential for business analytics)
- AP Calculus AB (required by most business schools)
- AP English Language (business communication)

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP Calculus BC (for finance/quantitative roles)
- AP Computer Science Principles (tech literacy)
- AP Psychology (consumer behavior, organizational psychology)
- Accounting courses
- AP Business with Personal Finance (launching 2026-2027)

**Recommended Extracurriculars**:
- DECA (marketing, finance, management competitions)
- FBLA (Future Business Leaders of America)
- Wharton Global High School Investment Competition
- Student-run business or startup
- Investment club
- National Economics Challenge
- Junior Achievement programs
- Entrepreneurship competitions
- Internships at local businesses

**Testing Priorities**: SAT/ACT with strong math + AP Economics scores.

**Course Sequence**:

| Year | Standard Path | Accelerated Path |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **9th** | Algebra I/Geometry, English, Elective | Honors Geometry, Honors English |
| **10th** | Algebra II, AP Human Geography or AP Econ | Pre-Calculus Honors, AP Microeconomics |
| **11th** | Pre-Calculus, AP Macro/Micro, AP Eng Lang | AP Calculus AB, AP Macroeconomics, AP Statistics |
| **12th** | AP Calculus AB, AP Statistics, AP Eng Lang | AP Calculus BC, AP CSP, Business elective |

### Law / Political Science / Humanities

**Must-Have Courses**:
- AP U.S. History + AP World History (historical analysis and writing)
- AP English Language AND AP English Literature (reading and writing mastery)
- AP U.S. Government and Politics
- AP Comparative Government (if available)

**Valuable Additions**:
- AP European History
- AP Psychology or AP Human Geography
- AP Statistics (data literacy)
- Philosophy or Ethics courses
- Latin or foreign language (4 years recommended)
- Public Speaking / Debate courses

**Recommended Extracurriculars**:
- Mock Trial (simulates legal reasoning — top choice)
- Debate team (critical thinking, argumentation)
- Model UN (diplomacy, global issues)
- Student Government
- School newspaper / journalism
- Community advocacy / volunteering
- Political campaigns or civic organizations
- Writing competitions
- Youth court / teen court programs

**Testing Priorities**: SAT/ACT with strong reading/writing scores + AP Humanities scores.

**Course Sequence**:

| Year | Standard Path | Accelerated Path |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **9th** | English 9 Honors, World Language I, Elective | Honors English, AP Human Geography |
| **10th** | AP World History, English 10 Honors, WL II | AP World History, Honors English, WL II |
| **11th** | AP U.S. History, AP English Language, WL III | APUSH, AP English Lang, AP Gov, WL III |
| **12th** | AP Government, AP English Lit, AP Euro, WL IV | AP Comp Gov, AP English Lit, AP Euro, WL IV |

**Important note**: There is no "pre-law" major in college. Law schools accept all majors. The key skills are reading comprehension, analytical writing, and logical reasoning — which is what the LSAT tests.

**Must-Have Courses**:

- Digital media / graphic design courses

## Trades / CTE Track

**This is NOT a lesser path.** Skilled trades earn a median salary of $55,680 (above the national median), and many trades earn $65,000-$100,000+. The counselor must present this path with equal respect and enthusiasm.

### CTE Career Clusters

CTE (Career and Technical Education) programs cover 16 career clusters: Agriculture, Architecture, Arts/AV, Business, Education, Finance, Government, Health Science, Hospitality, Human Services, IT, Law/Public Safety, Manufacturing, Marketing, STEM, Transportation. Many lead to industry certifications before graduation. Some include paid apprenticeships.

### Popular Trade Paths

| Trade | Training Time | Median Salary | Pathway |
|-------|--------------|---------------|---------|
| Electrician | 4-5 years (apprenticeship) | $65,000+ | CTE, Apprenticeship |
| Plumber | 4-5 years (apprenticeship) | $60,000+ | CTE, Apprenticeship |
| HVAC Technician | 6 months - 2 years | $55,000+ | CTE, Trade school |
| Welder | 7 months - 2 years | $50,000+ | CTE, Certification |
| Dental Hygienist | 2-3 years | $80,000+ | CTE, Associate degree |
| EMT/Paramedic | 6 months - 2 years | $45,000-65,000 | CTE, Certification |
| Automotive Tech | 1-2 years | $50,000+ | CTE, Trade school |
| Cybersecurity Tech | 1-2 years | $60,000+ | CTE, Certification |

### Advantages Over 4-Year College

- Little to no student debt
- Start earning 2-4 years earlier
- High demand (labor shortages in many trades)
- Hands-on work vs. classroom learning
- Many trades lead to self-employment and business ownership

### Counselor Guidance

When a student expresses interest in trades or CTE:
- Validate the choice with specific data (salaries, demand, career progression)
- Help them identify CTE programs at their school or regional career centers
- Discuss certification timelines and apprenticeship opportunities
- Explore whether a 2-year associate degree adds value for their chosen field
- Never frame trades as a fallback — frame them as a strategic career choice

## Math Sequence Guidance

The math sequence is the most common source of track misalignment. Getting it right early creates options; falling behind limits them.

| Track | Minimum Math | Recommended Math | Ideal Math |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Engineering / Physics / CS | AP Calculus AB | AP Calculus BC | AP Calc BC + Multivariable (dual enrollment) |
| Pre-Med / Bio | AP Calculus AB | AP Calculus AB + AP Statistics | AP Calculus BC + AP Statistics |
| Business / Economics | AP Calculus AB | AP Calculus AB + AP Statistics | AP Calculus BC + AP Statistics |
| Humanities / Social Science | Pre-Calculus | AP Statistics | Pre-Calculus + AP Statistics |
| Arts / Design | Algebra II | Pre-Calculus | Pre-Calculus + AP Statistics (for design thinking) |
| Trades / CTE | Algebra II | Geometry + applied math | Varies by trade |

### Standard vs. Accelerated vs. STEM-Bound

| Year | Standard | Accelerated | STEM-Bound |
|------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **9th** | Algebra I | Geometry | Algebra II / Honors Geometry |
| **10th** | Geometry | Algebra II | Pre-Calculus Honors |
| **11th** | Algebra II | Pre-Calculus | AP Calculus AB or BC |
| **12th** | Pre-Calculus | AP Calculus AB | AP Calculus BC, AP Stats, or Multivariable |

If a student is on the standard track but targeting a STEM field, explore summer coursework or dual enrollment to accelerate without overloading the school year.

## Dual Enrollment Recommendations

Dual enrollment lets students take college courses while in high school. Recommend it when:

- **School lacks advanced courses**: Student has exhausted AP offerings in their area of interest
- **Exploring a major**: Student wants to test a field before committing (e.g., intro philosophy, intro engineering)
- **Accelerating math**: School doesn't offer beyond AP Calculus AB, but student needs BC or Multivariable
- **Getting ahead**: Student wants to earn transferable college credit (often free or reduced cost)
- **Showing initiative**: Dual enrollment demonstrates academic ambition to admissions officers

**Important**: Check transfer policies — most public universities accept dual enrollment credits, but policies vary by school and state. UC system gives extra GPA weight for dual enrollment.

## Extracurricular Strategy: The Spike Model

### Depth Over Breadth

Top colleges want "well-lopsided," not "well-rounded." Admissions data confirms that sustained engagement, leadership, and measurable impact in 2-3 areas outweighs a long list of memberships.

- **The old model**: Join 10 clubs, do a bit of everything
- **The new model**: Deep commitment to 2-3 areas with demonstrated impact and growth

### Building a Spike

1. **Choose 1-2 core activities** aligned with your interest/academic track
2. **Show progression**: Member, Leader, Founder/Competitor/Creator
3. **Create measurable impact**: Start something, win something, change something
4. **Be authentic**: The spike should reflect genuine passion, not resume padding

### Four-Tier Activity Ranking

| Tier | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Tier 1: National/International** | Rare, national/international achievement | Intel Science Fair winner, USAMO qualifier, published research, national team |
| **Tier 2: State/Regional** | High achievement, significant leadership | Student body president, state competition winner, founded organization with measurable impact |
| **Tier 3: School/Institutional** | Meaningful involvement with some distinction | Club officer, varsity sport captain, Eagle Scout, regional competition finalist |
| **Tier 4: Community/Participation** | Solid participation | Club member, volunteer work, part-time job, school sports |

Most students have Tier 3-4 activities. Even one Tier 2 activity sets you apart. A Tier 1 activity is a significant differentiator.

### Spike Progression by Year

- **Freshman**: Join and explore — try 3-4 activities to discover what resonates
- **Sophomore**: Narrow to 2-3 core activities — seek increasing responsibility (committee chair, section leader)
- **Junior**: Lead and create impact — leadership roles, competitions, awards, projects with measurable outcomes
- **Senior**: Culminate — capstone projects, mentoring younger students, sustained meaningful involvement
